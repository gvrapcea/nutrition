Instrucțiuni

Folosiți cea mai bună estimare a alimentelor tipice pe care le consumați săptămânal. Pentru alimentele pe care le consumați ocazional, utilizați cea mai bună estimare a consumului dvs. pe parcursul unei luni sau unui an. Dacă vrei doar feedback cu privire la dieta ta în ultima săptămână sau lună, este bine și asta. Dar, ia întotdeauna în considerare atât cantitatea , cât și frecvența cu care mănânci un aliment în medie.

Beta-carotene

×
A brightly colored plant pigment in the carotenoid family with antioxidant properties. It is a precursor of vitamin A. Good sources include sweet potatoes; carrots; dark, leafy greens; winter squashes (acorn or butternut squash); apricots; broccoli; asparagus and grapefruit.

Lycopene

×
A bright red plant pigment in the carotenoid family with antioxidant properties. Good sources include tomatoes, watermelon, papayas, grapefruit and red peppers.

Isothiocyanates

×
Sulfur-containing compounds that lend some of the pungent flavor and smell to cruciferous vegetables. They are thought to help clear carcinogens from the body. Good sources include cabbage, mustard greens, Brussels sprouts, broccoli, bok choy, collard greens, kale and cauliflower.

Alpha-carotene

×
A brightly colored plant pigment in the carotenoid family with antioxidant properties. It is a precursor of vitamin A. Good sources are carrots, winter squashes (acorn or butternut squash), navy beans, tomatoes and tangerines.

Lutein and zeaxanthin

×
Brightly colored plant pigments in the carotenoid family with antioxidant properties. Good sources include dark, leafy greens; green peas; summer squashes (yellow and zucchini squash); broccoli; Brussels sprouts; green onions; corn and asparagus.

Anthocyanins

×
Red, purple or blue plant pigments with antioxidant properties in the flavonoid family. Good sources include purple cabbage, berries, radishes, plums, pistachios, nectarines, pears and peaches.

Luteolin

×
A flavonoid that is part of a plant's defenses against insects and UV radiation. It is thought to have anti-inflammatory properties. Good sources include peppers, olives, citrus fruits, celery, kiwi, broccoli and melon.
